## Grind Setting Guidance by Burr Size

General starting points for espresso on a Decent DE1 (target ~25-30s, 2:1 ratio):

| Burr Size | Typical Espresso Range | Adjustment Sensitivity | Notes |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|-------|
| 38-40mm | Very fine end of range | High - small changes matter | Hand grinders: each click can shift shot time 3-5s |
| 48mm | Fine, but more forgiving | Medium | Hand grinders: 1-2 clicks between good shots |
| 54mm | Medium-fine area | Medium | Good entry point for home espresso |
| 58mm | Medium-fine | Medium | Classic commercial size |
| 64mm | Mid-range for grinder | Medium-low | Most popular size; aftermarket burr options |
| 75-83mm | Mid-range, generous | Low - very small changes | Forgiving; large burrs = more uniform particles |
| 98mm | Wide sweet spot | Very low | EK43-class; very uniform; wide acceptable range |

## Burr Type Flavor Characteristics

| Burr Type | Espresso Character | Best For | Trade-offs |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|------------|
| Conical (traditional) | Full body, chocolate, nuts, classic | Medium-dark roasts, milk drinks | Less clarity on light roasts |
| Flat (espresso geometry) | Balanced, sweet, full body | All roasts, traditional espresso | Can clog with very light roasts |
| Flat (multipurpose) | Medium clarity, versatile | Switching between espresso and filter | Jack of all trades |
| Flat (unimodal/brew) | High clarity, fruity, tea-like | Light roast espresso, filter | Can taste thin with dark roasts |
| Hybrid (Versalab/Titus) | Complex, layered, unique | Specialty espresso | Expensive; limited availability |
